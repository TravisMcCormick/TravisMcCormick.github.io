Custom Corne Keyboard

2024

The Story Behind the Build

After spending countless hours coding and working on cybersecurity projects, I started experiencing early signs of carpal tunnel syndrome. Rather than accepting it as an inevitable consequence of my work, I decided to solve the problem myself.

I discovered the world of ergonomic split keyboards and decided to build my own Corne keyboard from scratch. This project became a perfect intersection of learning and problem-solving: I taught myself how to solder PCBs, dove deep into keyboard firmware (ZMK), designed custom keymaps optimized for my workflow, and even got into 3D printing to create custom cases and a carrying case.

The result? Not just a keyboard, but a completely personalized tool that reduces strain, increases my typing efficiency, and serves as a constant reminder that the best solutions often come from building them yourself.

Zettelkasten Knowledge Base

Ongoing

My Personal Knowledge Management System

In cybersecurity and technology, the amount of information I need to retain is overwhelming. Rather than relying on scattered notes or bookmarks, I implemented a Zettelkasten system—a method of interconnected note-taking that mimics how our brains naturally form connections between ideas.

My Zettelkasten repository is a living, evolving collection of notes on everything I learn: networking protocols, firewall configurations, lockpicking techniques, security vulnerabilities, hardware concepts, and more. Each note is atomic (one idea per note) and linked to related concepts, creating a web of knowledge I can navigate and reference whenever I need it.

This isn't just note-taking—it's building a second brain. It's how I stay sharp, make connections between disparate concepts, and ensure that what I learn today is still accessible years from now.

Lifeguard Scheduling App

January 2024 – May 2025

Solving a Real-World Problem

After six years as a lifeguard—starting at age 16, becoming a Water Safety Instructor, and eventually working my way up to Head Lifeguard—I noticed a persistent inefficiency: rotation scheduling was still being managed with dry erase boards.

When I started working for the City of Lakeland, I saw an opportunity to modernize this process. I developed a React web application that replaced the physical board with a mobile-friendly solution. Lifeguards could pull up the schedule on their phones, managers could make real-time changes without erasing and rewriting, and everyone stayed synchronized.

Why I Built It: This project represented everything I value—taking a problem I understand deeply from years of hands-on experience and applying my technical skills to create something that makes people's jobs easier. It's about bringing efficiency and modern tools to an industry that doesn't always get the tech attention it deserves.

Unfortunately, when I left the City of Lakeland upon graduation, the IT department chose not to maintain the application. While it never reached its full potential, the experience of collaborating with the superintendent, understanding user needs, and delivering a working solution taught me invaluable lessons about real-world software development.
